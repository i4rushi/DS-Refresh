- Built for linear structures (arrays, linked lists, strings)
- Allows for complexity reduction to O(n)
    - rather than O(n^2) time complexity in brute force solutions

-Two Pointers in the same direction
    - good for when you are scanning the data in a single pass
    - ex. fast and slow 
        - used to detect cycles in a linked list or to find the middle
            - cycle if at any point slow == fast
            - middle is at slow when fast reaches the end (in even cases slow ends up at second middle of list)
        - slow moves one step at a time 
        - fast moves two steps

-Two Pointers in the opposite direction
    - good for finding pairs / comparing elems at opposite ends of the data structure
    - ex. two nums that sum to target value
        - eliminates unecessary comparisons
    - check current condition at opposite ends
    - move one pointer inward if condition not matched
    - each pointer moves one at a time

    - especially powerful in sorted arrays

- Two pointes benefit by: (time and space optimization)
    - reducing iterations
    - tracking relation between two places
    - avoiding extra space 

- Example Problems:
    - Palindromes, Reversals, merging sorted data, K sized comparisons
